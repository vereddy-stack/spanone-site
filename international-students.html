FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is college planning important?

College admissions have become very competitive, and admission officers are looking for more than just grades and test scores. An expert college admissions consultant and early college planning can help you reach your college goals — including a vast array of scholarship and financial aid opportunities.

Who are SpanOne advisors?

SpanOne advisors are a network of qualified college admissions counselors and independent college planning consultants across the United States. Many are former admissions staff, graduates, and other educational professionals from America's top colleges, bringing a personalized approach to each student and parent.

How will a SpanOne Advisor help me?

Your Advisor will engage you in conversations about goals for college and career, help you build a strong high-school resume, guide you step-by-step through applications, and help you keep track of important dates and deadlines.

What are the working hours of SpanOne Advisors?

SpanOne Advisors are based in the US. They are happy to be flexible with respect to international time differences.

How are personal sessions with the advisor conducted?

Personal sessions with the advisor are done online using Skype or Zoom.

What are the language proficiency requirements to study in the U.S.?

Undergraduate programs in the U.S. are taught in English, and colleges typically require proof of proficiency — most commonly the TOEFL or IELTS, which assess reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills.

What are the standardized testing requirements for international students?

The SAT and ACT are the two main standardized tests accepted by U.S. colleges and universities. While some colleges are test-optional, many still consider scores as part of admissions, especially for international applicants.

What financial aid and scholarships are available to international students?

Many colleges and universities offer financial aid and scholarships to international students, though availability and criteria vary. Our counselors work with students to investigate these opportunities and advise on submitting applications by deadline.

What are visa requirements to study in the U.S.?

The most common student visa for international undergraduates is the F-1 visa, which requires proof of acceptance to a U.S. institution and demonstrated financial ability to cover tuition and living expenses. It's crucial to start the process well in advance, as timing can vary.

Does SpanOne partner with international high schools?

Yes — SpanOne partners with international high schools to help students reach their higher education goals in the US, connecting them with an experienced college counselor. Email us for our partnership brochure.
