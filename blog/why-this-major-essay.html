How to Write the “Why this Major” Essay

← Back to Blog
April 2024

Many colleges require a supplemental essay that asks applicants to explain why they have chosen their major.

Writing the "Why This Major" Essay

Your answer needs to be in sync with what is reflected in the rest of your application. For example, if your personal statement talks about your goal to be a filmmaker, and you indicate that you want your major to be aerospace engineering, it may appear there's a disconnect unless you can explain the connection.

Write about what attracts you to the major you're choosing. Is there a class that inspired you, a skill you learned at school or on your own, or a job shadow or internship that sparked your interest?

How to Structure the Essay

A good way to structure the essay is to begin with a statement about the major you're interested in, followed by examples of classes or activities that inspired you, details about those experiences and how they impacted you, and what you learned. The essay can conclude with the type of future work you envision, or how you hope to help your town, city, or the world.

Other areas to refer to when discussing why you've chosen a specific major:

  • Specific courses offered by the college you're applying to
  • Professors you would like to study under
  • Research projects related to the major
  • Study abroad opportunities

The above information can be found by reviewing a college's website. When reviewing your essay, ask yourself if it truly communicates that you've put thought into choosing your major, and if it demonstrates a strong interest in pursuing it.
